Methodology Pipeline

1. Data Integration

The model integrates data from multiple sources to characterize the transmission niche:

  • Environmental: Land use (Forest, Pasture, Urban) from MapBiomas; Climate (Temperature, Precipitation) from WorldClim.
  • Demographic: Absolute Population (IBGE) and Density.
  • Immunological: Vaccination coverage (PNI/DATASUS) and estimated population at risk.

2. Feature Engineering & Pre-processing

  • Normalization of continuous variables (StandardScaler).
  • One-Hot Encoding for categorical regions/clusters.
  • Imputation of missing values (Median strategy).
  • Generation of "Forest Loss Proxy" and "Unvaccinated Risk" indices.

3. Future Scenarios (2021–2040)

Future risk is estimated by applying mathematical transformations to the baseline data, simulating different policy and environmental outcomes.

Scenario Description & Assumptions
A - Climate Only (SSP2-4.5) Simulates climate change trends: Temp +1.5°C, Precipitation -5%, and VPD +5%. All other factors remain constant.
B - Vaccination Up Climate change (A) + 20% increase in vaccination coverage (capped at 100%).
C - Vaccination Down Climate change (A) + 20% decrease in vaccination coverage.
D - Population Growth Climate change (A) + 15% increase in absolute population.
F - Agro-Expansion Climate change (A) + Pasture (+20%), Urban (+10%), and Forest (-5%).
G - Conservation Climate change (A) + Forest (+10%) and Pasture (-10%).

Feature Importance

The model identified the following drivers as the most influential in predicting Yellow Fever occurrence (based on tree-based feature importance):

  • Demography: Population size and density are critical scaling factors for transmission risk.
  • Vaccination: Low coverage areas (high percentage of unvaccinated individuals) are strong predictors of outbreaks.
  • Climate: Maximum temperature and precipitation seasonality define the mosquito vector suitability.
  • Land Use: Proximity to forest edges and fragmented landscapes (Forest/Pasture interface) increases sylvatic spillover risk.
