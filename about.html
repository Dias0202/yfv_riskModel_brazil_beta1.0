Data Sources & Acquisition

The dataset integrates epidemiological, environmental, and demographic variables at the municipality level across Brazil. Data was aggregated into 5-year clusters (2001–2020) to capture temporal trends.

1. Epidemiological Data

Yellow Fever Occurrences (Target Variable): Binary classification (0/1) indicating the presence of confirmed Yellow Fever cases (human or epizootic) in a municipality-period.

  • Source: Ministry of Health / SINAN (Sistema de Informação de Agravos de Notificação).
  • Reference: [INSERT LINK TO DATASUS/SINAN]
  • Processing: Data cleaned to remove inconsistencies; aggregated by municipality and cluster period.

2. Environmental Predictors

Climatic Variables: Monthly averages for Maximum Temperature, Minimum Temperature, Precipitation, and Vapor Pressure Deficit (VPD).

  • Source: WorldClim v2.1 (Historical) / Mathematical Simulation based on MIROC6 SSP2-4.5 (Future).
  • Reference: [INSERT LINK TO WORLDCLIM]
  • Resolution: ~1km spatial resolution, aggregated to municipality mean.

Land Use & Cover: Percentage of municipality area covered by Forest, Pasture, and Urban infrastructure.

  • Source: MapBiomas Collection [INSERT VERSION, e.g., 6.0 or 7.0].
  • Reference: [INSERT LINK TO MAPBIOMAS]
  • Processing: Normalized area calculation (km²) relative to total municipality size.

3. Demographic & Vaccination Data

Population: Absolute population counts and density. IBGE

Vaccination Coverage: Estimated coverage of Yellow Fever vaccine in the target population. PNI / DATASUS

Assumptions & Limitations

While this model provides a robust framework for risk assessment, the following limitations should be considered:

  • Underreporting: Historical data depends on notification systems. Areas with weak surveillance may have false negatives (recorded as 0, but had undiagnosed cases).
  • Spatial Aggregation: Using municipality-level means can mask local heterogeneities (e.g., a municipality might be 90% urban but have a small, high-risk forest fringe).
  • Future Simulations: The scenarios for 2021–2040 are mathematical simulations of sensitivity based on the SSP2-4.5 trends, not dynamic biological forecasts. They assume stationarity in the relationship between climate and transmission.
  • Vaccination Data: Coverage estimates can exceed 100% or contain errors due to population mobility and census inaccuracies. We capped coverage at 1.0 (100%) for modeling purposes.
